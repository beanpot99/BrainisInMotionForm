The PDMS-2nd Edition is a criterion-referenced assessment, which measures fine and gross motor skill development from birth through age 6. Each test item is scored as a 0,1, or 2 with the total raw score for each section calculated by adding these points. Raw scores are converted to standard scores and age equivalents. Age Equivalent is the least reliable score. The Fine Motor Quotient(FMQ) is derived by adding the standard scores from the two fine motor sections. Scores that fall between 90-110 are considered in the average range for this test, with a standard deviation of 15.
